The Maya Block 5 rocket is set to lift off NET June 07, 11:10 UTC at Bataan Space Center. Window lasts upto 110 minutes with backup on the 9th and 10th.

The Maya family of rockets evolve faster as our teams gather more data per flights. Through extensive testing of the spacecraft's attitude on ascent until landing, we have upgraded for a more safer, more powerful and human-rated booster, the Block 5.

Lessons learned from the previous 7 flights were obtained, including the experimental landing tests downrange either the sea or by land, and a single RTLS attempt on flight 6. This mission will feature an enhanced Guidance and Navigation Systems (GNS) that guides Maya on a nominal descent path to landing. This includes the precision orbit and landing on both the vehicles.

Flight 8, which we will call Experimental Landing Mission - 1 (EXT-1). This mission's flight objectives is to nominally launch Maya 5 into space, and put our customer payload @OmegaLink to orbit.

Before we proceed, we updated several Maya hardwares, and the most noticeable is its size. Block 5 introduces 75% of Maya Block 4's height, for a more shorter fuel capacity.

Since we shortened the size of the fuel tanks, we made our engine, Agila Engine more powerful producing 2,000 kN of thrust each. With a more cleaner CH2 LOX engine, our rocket is environmental friendly.
